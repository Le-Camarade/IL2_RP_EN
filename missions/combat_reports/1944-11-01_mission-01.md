## A — Sortie Detail

**Mission:** Contact Patrol — contact reconnaissance, front line establishment
**Take-off time:** 14:30
**Landing time:** approx. 16:15
**Base:** Volkel (B-80), Netherlands

## B — Narrative

Weather conditions deteriorated from take-off. Total cloud cover, continuous rain, visibility reduced to a few hundred yards in places. The P-51B-5 escort from the 352nd Fighter Group (four aircraft) was lost to sight shortly after Volkel in the low cloud layer. Intermittent visual contact maintained with two escort elements on passing Gorinchem.

The pilot conducted the reconnaissance at approximately 2,000 feet below the cloud base, in accordance with orders. Over the Gorinchem sector, a large fire was observed at the port installations: barges and port infrastructure ablaze, flames visible for several miles despite the rain. Origin and time of outbreak undetermined.

The positions of friendly troops along the front line could not be established. Weather conditions — rain, ground fog — rendered ground observation practically impossible. No concentration of friendly troops was visually confirmed in the assigned sector.

Enemy flak active at several points along the route: heavy fire over 's-Hertogenbosch en route to the objective; dense flak over Gorinchem — the aircraft took splinter hits on passing. No enemy aircraft were sighted.

Return to Volkel with two escort elements visible at landing. Two P-51s from the 352nd FG did not return — contact lost early in the mission due to weather conditions. Matter referred to the American liaison officer.
