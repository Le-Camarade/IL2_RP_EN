# BRIEFING — 326 Squadron RAF
**Date:** 2 November 1944 | **Take-off:** 09:30 | **Base:** Volkel (B-80), Netherlands

---

## General Situation

The Canadians are pushing towards the Hollands Diep. The Germans are pulling back, leaving equipment on the roads — convoys on the move, ground targets are priority. Weather conditions markedly improved compared to yesterday.

## Mission

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Type** | Ground Hunt — free-ranging transport sweep |
| **Objective** | Locate and destroy enemy transport convoys (trucks, motorised equipment) on road axes in the sector |
| **Area** | Road axes, Noord-Brabant sector |
| **Altitude** | 3,900 feet (search) — attack passes at visual range |

## Weather

- Cover: light cloud
- Cloud base: **12,200 feet**
- Wind: 22° / 4 mph (near calm)
- Visibility: good — window until noon

## Expected Opposition

Light embedded flak on convoys (MG-34, 20mm). Approach from the beam; do not dive along the column axis. Enemy fighters possible — JG1 and JG26 active last night over Eindhoven. P-47s providing high cover.

## Instructions

- Diversion airfield: Eindhoven (B-78)
- Return before 11:30
- One clean pass is worth more than three passes under fire
- Chevalier and Vitry: follow, do not engage independently
