# BRIEFING — 326 Squadron RAF
**Date:** 1 November 1944 | **Take-off:** 14:30 | **Base:** Volkel (B-80), Netherlands

---

## General Situation

Post-Market Garden. The 2nd Canadian Army is clearing Noord-Brabant; the line on the Meuse has not yet been stabilised. Ground intelligence is running 24 hours behind — hence this mission. Total overcast, rain, possible ground fog on return. Wind NNW, 16 mph.

## Mission

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Type** | Contact Patrol — contact reconnaissance |
| **Objective** | Establish the position of friendly concentrations near Gouda. Confirm the front line. |
| **Area** | Gouda sector, South Holland |
| **Altitude** | 2,000 feet |

## Flight Composition

| Position | Pilot | Unit |
|----------|--------|-------|
| Contact patrol | **S/L Falstaff** | 326 Sqn |
| Top cover (×4) | G. Preddy (lead) | 352nd FG — P-51B-5 |

## Weather

- Cover: total overcast, rain
- Cloud base: **3,700 feet**
- Wind: 344° / 16 mph
- Ground fog on return probable

## Expected Opposition

JG1 and JG26 active in the sector — Fw 190 A-8 and Bf 109 K-4. Diving attacks from the cloud layer. Light to moderate flak over Gouda.

## Instructions

- Diversion airfield: Eindhoven (B-78)
- Return before 16:30 — dusk 17:00
- If escort is lost: climb through cloud layer, heading 220° back to base
- Weather window: 2 hours
